Hi!
I am Neelabja (nee.lab.joe),
aspiring Social Cognition researcher

Nature and its complex components like primates (us) and society fascinate me.
Thus, I love to philosophise and work on social cognition: intergroup behaviour (cooperation vs discrimination), social intelligence, socioeconomics, political psych, developmental cognition, complex systems and emergence, political theories, governance and administration, and their related models.

Parallel to the generally capitalistic notion of specialisation, I would like to see myself being a Jack of most trades. Somehow, I have also managed to become a decent 'Master' of none Science (by Research) in Cognitive Science from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. Now, I am charting my next steps, ever so gradually to seek and find what lies next.

Self Reflection

You are definitely acquainted with a particular figure of speech, which like most other famous ones, is mostly misquoted and misinterpreted, often quite widely so!
The entire saying actually reads, "Jack of all, master of none, but oftentimes better, than a master of one".
That, I find, is surprisingly meaningful to my own being. Why? I believe that any truth, if it is indeed objectively true, can be reached at with multiple paths of search.
As someone who is trying to become well rounded in the interdisciplinary Cognitive perspective of understanding our brain, mind and existence, I have been picking up research experiences from different approaches of the famed hexagon . This can be summarised as an attempt to become a jack of most trades of the craft, is it not?

I am currently a Research Data Analyst with Dr Urvakhsh Mehta at the Social Cognition & Schizophrenia Lab at the National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS), India . I am also collaborating in research projects with Dr Christopher Krupenye, of the Social Origins Lab at Johns Hopkins Univ, with Dr Pragathi Priyadarshani at the Translational Neuroscience lab at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur.

Earlier, I have been professionally engaged as a lead Project Associate with Dr Ark Verma's Lab at IIT Kanpur, and as a research analyst with IndianPAC before that.

Academically, my constant confusion with my own existence had landed me at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur as a Cognitive Science MS(by Research) student. It was an unparalleled experience of learning and growing as a serious academic researcher.
Before that, my undergraduate training in B.Tech Computer Science & Engineering graduate from HIT Kolkata. The experience was sparsed with enriching lessons from a few extraordinary beings.

I could not have asked for a better schooling experience for 12 years at my second home, South Point High School.

In the midst of academic endeavours, I have been indulging in a political cognition & public policy researcher professionally as well as a hobby.
Football, along with trying to stargaze underneath polluted skies count as my leisure.
