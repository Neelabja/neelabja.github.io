Hi hi!
I am Neelabja (nee.lab.joe),
an aspiring Social Cognition researcher

Nature and its complex components like primates (us) and society fascinate me.
Thus, I love to philosophise and work on social cognition: intergroup behaviour (cooperation vs discrimination), social intelligence, socioeconomics, political psych, developmental cognition, complex systems and emergence, political theories, governance and administration, and their related models.

Parallel to the generally capitalistic notion of specialisation, I would like to see myself being a Jack of most trades. Somehow, I have also managed to become a decent 'Master' of none Science (by Research) in Cognitive Science from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. Now, I am charting my next steps, ever so gradually to seek and find what lies next.

Self Reflection

You would have heard a quote, which like most other quotes, is often misquoted, widely so!

The actual saying in its entirety reads as 'Jack of all, master of none, but oftentimes better, than a master of one'

I find that immensely meaningful in line of my own lifeform. And the mission of someday becoming, a jack of most trades (? maybe, idk really)

My confusion with my own existence had gifted me a place at the IIT Kanpur as an MS(R) student at their Cognitive Science program.
I wont be able to put into words the experience it has been.

I had my undergraduate training as a B.Tech Computer Science and Engineering graduate from HITK.
The best I can say about it is that even in the haystack, I fortunately had stumbled upon some of the most extraordinary things and beings, who I think have changed everything for whom I am, the little I am, today.

Probably I define myself by putting out in my first breath that I didn't have a good home. But I got to experience atleast the taste of a better childhood and adolescence (Grades 1 to 12) at my second home, South Point High School, Kolkata
